## Introduction: Why Image Formats Matter

Images account for **over 50% of the average webpage's total size**. Choosing the wrong format can:
- Slow down your website by 2-3 seconds
- Increase bandwidth costs unnecessarily
- Hurt your Google rankings (page speed is a ranking factor)
- Frustrate mobile users on slow connections

The good news? Understanding image formats is simpler than you think.

<a name="jpeg"></a>
## JPEG (JPG) - The Universal Standard

### What is JPEG?

JPEG (Joint Photographic Experts Group) has been the dominant image format since 1992. It uses **lossy compression**, meaning some image data is permanently discarded to achieve smaller file sizes.

### Technical Details
- **Compression**: Lossy (removes data to reduce size)
- **Transparency**: Not supported
- **Best for**: Photographs, complex images with gradients
- **Browser Support**: 100% (works everywhere)

### When to Use JPG

✅ **Use JPG when:**
- Saving photographs or images with many colors
- File size is more important than perfect quality
- You don't need transparency
- Maximum compatibility is required

❌ **Avoid JPG for:**
- Logos or images with text (creates artifacts)
- Screenshots with sharp edges
- Images that need transparency
- Images that will be edited multiple times

### Real-World Example

A 5MB photo from your camera can be compressed to 500KB as JPG with barely noticeable quality loss - that's a **90% reduction**!

<a name="png"></a>
## PNG - The King of Transparency

### What is PNG?

PNG (Portable Network Graphics) was created as a patent-free alternative to GIF. It uses **lossless compression**, preserving every pixel perfectly.

### Technical Details
- **Compression**: Lossless (no quality loss)
- **Transparency**: Full alpha channel support
- **Best for**: Logos, graphics, screenshots
- **File Size**: Typically 2-5x larger than JPG

### When to Use PNG

✅ **Use PNG when:**
- You need transparency (e.g., logos over backgrounds)
- Working with graphics, icons, or text
- Image quality cannot be compromised
- Editing images multiple times

❌ **Avoid PNG for:**
- Photographs (files will be huge)
- When file size is critical
- Email attachments with size limits

### PNG-8 vs PNG-24

- **PNG-8**: 256 colors max, smaller files (good for simple graphics)
- **PNG-24**: 16 million colors, larger files (good for complex images)

<a name="webp"></a>
## WebP - The Modern Standard

### What is WebP?

Developed by Google in 2010, WebP delivers **superior compression** for both lossy and lossless images. It's the format Google wants everyone to use.

### Technical Details
- **Compression**: Both lossy and lossless modes
- **Transparency**: Yes (alpha channel)
- **File Size**: 25-35% smaller than JPG/PNG
- **Browser Support**: 95%+ (all modern browsers)

### Key Advantages

1. **Smaller Files**: WebP images are typically 26% smaller than PNG and 25-34% smaller than JPG at equivalent quality
2. **Versatility**: Supports both lossy and lossless compression
3. **Transparency**: Unlike JPG, WebP supports alpha channels
4. **Animation**: Can create animated images (like GIF but better)

### When to Use WebP

✅ **Use WebP when:**
- Building modern websites (with JPG fallback)
- Page speed is critical
- Targeting mobile users
- You want both small size AND good quality

❌ **Avoid WebP for:**
- Sharing images with non-tech-savvy users
- Email attachments
- Apps that may not support it
- When you need absolute universal compatibility

<a name="avif"></a>
## AVIF - The Future Contender

### What is AVIF?

AVIF (AV1 Image File Format) is the newest format, derived from the AV1 video codec. It promises even better compression than WebP.

### Technical Details
- **Compression**: Advanced (based on AV1 video codec)
- **File Size**: Up to 50% smaller than JPG
- **Quality**: Superior at low bitrates
- **Browser Support**: ~80% (growing rapidly)

### The AVIF Advantage

AVIF excels at:
- **Ultra-efficient compression**: 20% smaller than WebP
- **HDR support**: Wide color gamut
- **Film grain**: Preserves texture better
- **Future-proof**: Industry-backed standard

### Should You Use AVIF Now?

**Not yet** for most projects. While promising, AVIF has limitations:
- Slower encoding/decoding than WebP
- Limited browser support (no Safari on old macOS/iOS)
- Fewer tools support it

**Recommendation**: Stick with WebP for now, keep an eye on AVIF.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the highest quality image format?

**PNG** offers perfect lossless quality, but **TIFF** is the gold standard for professional photography. For web use, PNG is your best bet for maximum quality.

### Is WebP better than JPG?

**Yes, for web use**. WebP provides better compression (25-34% smaller) while maintaining similar visual quality. However, JPG is still better for universal compatibility.

### How do I change a picture to JPG on my phone?

**iPhone**: Use the Files app → Select image → Share → Save to Files → Change extension
**Android**: Use Google Photos → Edit → Save → Format → JPG

Or simply use our [online converter](/jpg-to-png) from your phone browser!

### Can I convert images without losing quality?

**Yes and no**. Converting from:
- PNG → PNG: No loss (lossless to lossless)
- JPG → PNG: No additional loss (already lossy)
- PNG → JPG: Quality loss (lossless to lossy)

**Best practice**: Always keep original files in lossless format.

### What format does Google prefer?

Google recommends **WebP** for modern websites, with **JPG/PNG fallbacks** for older browsers. For maximum SEO, prioritize page speed over perfect quality.
