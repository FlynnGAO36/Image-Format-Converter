## What is HEIC?

**HEIC** stands for **High Efficiency Image Container**. It's based on the HEIF (High Efficiency Image Format) standard developed by the MPEG group—the same organization behind MP3 and MP4 formats.

### Key Facts About HEIC:

- 📱 **Default format** on iPhones since iOS 11 (2017)
- 💾 **50% smaller** file sizes compared to JPG
- 🎨 **Better quality** at smaller file sizes
- 📸 Supports **advanced features** like Live Photos and depth maps
- 🔧 Based on **HEVC (H.265)** video compression technology

---

## Why Does Apple Use HEIC?

Apple switched to HEIC as the default photo format for several compelling reasons:

### 1. **Massive Storage Savings**

HEIC files are approximately **50% smaller** than equivalent JPG files while maintaining the same quality.

**Example:**
- JPG photo: 3 MB
- Same photo in HEIC: 1.5 MB
- **Savings: 50% storage space**

For iPhone users taking hundreds or thousands of photos:
- 1,000 JPG photos: ~3 GB
- 1,000 HEIC photos: ~1.5 GB
- **You save 1.5 GB of storage!**

### 2. **Superior Image Quality**

HEIC uses more advanced compression algorithms than the 30-year-old JPG format. This means:
- ✅ Less compression artifacts
- ✅ Better color preservation
- ✅ More detail retention
- ✅ Smoother gradients

### 3. **Advanced Features Support**

HEIC can store additional data that JPG cannot:

- **16-bit color** (vs JPG's 8-bit)
- **Transparency** (like PNG)
- **Image sequences** (Live Photos)
- **Depth maps** (for Portrait mode)
- **Edit history** (non-destructive editing)
- **Multiple images** in one file

### 4. **Modern Technology**

JPG was created in 1992. HEIC, developed in 2015, leverages decades of advancement in compression technology.
